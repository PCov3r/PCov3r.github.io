Let me introduce myself,

Hey there, my name is Paul Devouge. I am a PhD student in Particle Physics working full time at CEA Saclay. During my free time, I like to create things and I've worked on different projects, from weather stations to embedded Machine Learning or even robots.

I graduated from the Ecole Nationale Superieure de l'Electronique et de Ses Applications , a French engineering school specialized in electronics, computer science and telecommunications. During my third year, I studied at University College Cork, as part of a double degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering. My Master's Thesis was about parallel compliant robots, with the study and development of a 3RRR compliant planar parallel robot.

I specialized in electronic engineering. I have always been interested in Physics and Engineering and am currently doing research in particle physics, working on the CMS experiment at CERN. My thesis work consists both in a Physics analysis, and technical work. More precisely, I am working on the Level-1 Trigger for the CMS experiment, with embedded distributed neural networks on FPGAs.
