Let me introduce myself,

Hey there, my name is Paul Devouge. I am a PhD student working full time at CEA Saclay. I have always been interested in Physics and Engineering. My passion for electronics started years ago. I like creating things and I have done many projects, from weather stations to embedded Machine Learning or even robots.

I graduated from the Ecole Nationale Superieure de l'Electronique et Ses Applications, a French engineering school specialized in electronics, computer science and telecommunications. During my third year, I studied at University College Cork, as part of a double degree in electrical and electronic engineering. My Master's Thesis was about parallel compliant robots, with the study and development of a 3RRR compliant planar parallel robot.

I specialized in electronic engineering and am currently doing research in particle physics, working on the CMS experiment at CERN. My thesis work consists both in a Physics analysis, and technical work. More particularely, I am working on the Level-1 Trigger for the CMS experiment, with embedded distributed neural networks on FPGAs.
